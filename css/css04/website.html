사쿠라 (Sakura) is the Japanese term for cherry blossom trees and their blossoms. These beautiful flowers bloom in the spring and are a significant part of Japanese culture.

The tradition of hanami (flower viewing) involves gathering with friends and family to enjoy the transient beauty of cherry blossoms. This practice has been celebrated for centuries in Japan.

메인 컨텐츠

The cherry blossom, or sakura, holds a special place in Japanese culture. It symbolizes the fleeting nature of life, as the beautiful blooms only last for a short time. This aligns with the Buddhist concept of impermanence.

Every year, people across Japan eagerly await the cherry blossom forecast, which predicts when the sakura will be in full bloom. Popular hanami spots become crowded with people enjoying picnics under the cherry trees, often with bento boxes and sake.

Cherry blossoms are also a common motif in Japanese art, literature, and music. They appear in traditional and contemporary works, symbolizing beauty, renewal, and the ephemerality of life.

Sakura Blossom Tea is a delicacy enjoyed during the cherry blossom season. Made by steeping pickled cherry blossoms in hot water, it has a light, floral flavor and is often served at celebrations and special occasions to signify the arrival of spring.
